Most Common Symptoms

COVID-19 affects different people in different ways. Most infected people will develop mild to moderate illness and recover without hospitalization.

FEVER

Normal range: 97F - 99F (Ideal 98.6F)

Many infections can cause fever, but if a person has a fever of 100.4°F (38°C) or above, they should stay at home or get medical advice from doctor, This is because it could be an early symptom of COVID-19. It was present in 78% of cases.

DRY COUGH

60-70% Covid patient develop it

Mucus is not produced . May experience a dry, scratchy, sore, or rough throat. There may be a sensation of a tickle or a patch of the throat that feels dry, even when swallowing. There may not be any relief from a dry cough, even after coughing.

TIREDNESS

3rd most found symptom

According to report, Fatigue is the third most common symptom of COVID-19 with 38.1% of people reporting the symptom. The only two more common symptoms, according to that report, are fever (87.9%) and dry cough (67.7%).

Less Common Symptoms

People with mild symptoms who are otherwise healthy should manage their symptoms at home. On average it takes 5–6 days from when someone is infected with the virus for symptoms to show, however it can take up to 14 days.

ACHES & PAINS

Reasons: Dehydration, Lack of Sleep

Body aches are a common symptom of many conditions. The flu is one of the most well-known conditions that can cause body aches. Aches can also be caused by your everyday life, especially if you stand, walk, or exercise for long periods of time.

SORE THROAT

Causes 13 million visits to doctor each year

Sore throats are divided into 3 types, based on the part of the throat they affect:

  • 1. area right behind the mouth
  • 2. swelling and redness of the tonsils
  • 3. swelling and redness of the voice box, or larynx

DIARRHOEA

Symptoms: nausea, abdominal pain

Diarrhea is characterized by loose, watery stools or frequent need to have a bowel movement. You might experience diarrhea as a result of a viral/ bacterial infection. Other times, it could also be due to food poisoning.

HEADACHE

7/10 people have atleast a headache each year

Headache common symptoms include throbbing, squeezing, constant unrelenting/ intermittent. It may be in one part of the head or may be generalized involving the whole head. It may be due to migraine, cluster, and tension headaches.

LOSS OF TASTE & SMELL

Temporary loss of smell in 60% of cases

Your sense of taste and sense of smell are closely linked. It’s estimated that 95 percent of the time when there’s a loss of taste, it’s associated with a reduced sense of smell. It may occur without a runny or stuffy nose.

SKIN RASHES

Neck & exposed chest eczema

The rash appears as reddish and purplish bumps on the fingers or toes. The fingers and toes are usually sore, but not itchy. As the rash heals, the top layers of skin may peel away leaving scaly patches.Oral rash, a person's lips may feel sore.

Serious Symptoms

Seek immediate medical attention if you have serious symptoms. Always call before visiting your doctor or health facility.

BREATHING PROBLEMS

Immediately call the doctor/ medical helpline

Common causes of feeling short of breath are: lung problems (such as asthma and COPD), heart problems (such as a cardiovascular disease and heart failure), infections in the airways, such as croup, bronchitis, pneumonia, COVID-19.

CHEST PAIN

Immediately call the doctor/ medical helpline

Don't play doctor, go see one, fast. If you are worried about pain/ discomfort in chest, upper back, left arm, or jaw; or suddenly faint or develop a cold sweat, nausea, or vomiting. Call local emergency number to summon an emergency medical crew.
