1. Face Mask

Wear face covering/ mask to cover your mouth and nose when around others and when you go out in public. The mask is meant to protect other people in case you are infected. Don’t place one on young children under age 2, anyone who has trouble breathing, or is unconscious or otherwise unable to remove the mask without assistance. N95 and Surgical Mask are the best.

2. Wash Hand regularly

Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, especially after being in a public place, or after blowing your nose, coughing or sneezing. If soap and water are not readily available, use a hand sanitizer with at least 60%-70% alcohol.

3. Social Distancing

Avoid close contact with people who are sick, and practice social distancing by keeping at least 6 feet away from others if you must go out in public.

4. Don't touch your:
Eyes, Nose & Mouth

Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth with unwashed hands while working in office or even while working at home.

5. Cover your face while
Sneezing/ Coughing

Cover your nose and mouth with a tissue when coughing or sneezing and throw the tissue away after use. If a tissue isn’t available, cough or sneeze into your elbow or sleeve, not your hands.

6. Clean and disinfect surfaces
around home & work

Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces daily. This includes tables, doorknobs, light switches, handles, desks, computers, phones, keyboards, sinks, toilets, faucets and countertops.
